# Roomly — TVC Creative Brief
**Campaign**: Room for You  
**Formats**: :30 hero · :15 cut-down · :06 bumper  
**Tone**: Warm, honest, satisfying — real people, real budgets, real results  

---

## The Insight

Most room-makeover content is either:
- Too expensive to relate to (magazine shoots, designer budgets)
- Too vague to act on (Pinterest mood boards, generic AI concepts)

Roomly is neither. It shows you exactly what YOUR room can look like with YOUR money — and then lets you buy it today.

**Emotional promise**: That quiet click of satisfaction when something finally fits into place. Not "wow, I wish I could afford that." Just: *yes, that's it.*

---

## Campaign Line

> **"Design a room you love. On your budget."**

Secondary: *Your room. Transformed.*

---

## Audience

- **Primary**: Women 22–34, renters or first-home owners, urban/suburban
- **Secondary**: Couples 25–38 furnishing a new place on a joint budget
- **Mindset**: Design-curious but overwhelmed. Know what they DON'T want, unsure how to get what they DO want. Budget-conscious, not budget-shameful.

---

## Concept: "The Click"

A single, uninterrupted 30 seconds that follows one person — from a feeling of low-level dissatisfaction with their room, to the quiet satisfaction of a plan that actually fits their life.

No dramatic before/after reveal. No shock edit. Just a warm, real moment: *this is possible. Today. For me.*

## :30 Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1 — THE STUCK MOMENT `(0:00 – 0:05)`
**Shot**: Wide. Living room, afternoon. Soft natural light through a curtained window.  
**Action**: A young woman (late 20s) sits sideways on a couch, one knee up, looking at her room. Not unhappy — just... stuck. The room is functional but soulless: mismatched pieces, bare walls, a rug that doesn't quite fit. She exhales slowly.  
**Audio**: Ambient only. Faint street sound. No VO.  
**Feeling**: Relatable. Not tragic. The quiet "I know this could be better."

---

### Scene 2 — THE PHOTO `(0:05 – 0:08)`
**Shot**: MCU → OTS as she raises her phone.  
**Action**: She opens Roomly, points the camera at the room, and taps. Casual. Like she's done it a hundred times before.  
**VO** *(soft, conversational)*: *"Upload a photo of your room."*  
**Visual**: Phone screen visible in frame — Roomly's clean UI, camera viewfinder with soft terracotta border.

---

### Scene 3 — THE SETUP `(0:08 – 0:13)`
**Shot**: CU phone screen, her hand navigating. Clean OTS angle.  
**Action**: She types her budget — **$320** — and selects AUD. Taps Continue. The setup form appears: room type auto-filled as "Living room," style preference "warm minimalist."  
**VO**: *"Set your budget. Tell us your style."*  
**Visual**: UI transitions are smooth (Lenis scroll). Budget field glows terracotta on focus.

---

### Scene 4 — THE QUIZ `(0:13 – 0:17)`
**Shot**: CU phone screen + insert of her face reacting in soft focus behind it.  
**Action**: Quiz cards appear — mood images, palette options. She taps through them quickly, instinctively. The card she picks gets a terracotta ring. She's not overthinking it.  
**VO**: *"Answer five quick questions."*  
**Visual**: Each quiz card snap is a real image (woven textures, terracotta palette, cozy zoned layout). Snappy, confident taps.

---

### Scene 5 — THE WAIT `(0:17 – 0:21)`
**Shot**: MCU — her face. Soft, curious. Phone glow on her face.  
**Action**: The generating screen is live. A subtle Sparkles icon rotates. Progress steps tick through: "Designing your look… Rendering the vision…" She watches. Leans in slightly.  
**VO**: *"Roomly designs your room — with real products from stores you already use."*  
**Visual**: Phone screen briefly visible: AliExpress, IKEA, Kmart, Amazon, Taobao logos in retailer row.

---

### Scene 6 — THE REVEAL `(0:21 – 0:27)`
**Shot**: CU phone screen → match cut to the actual room (wide).  
**Action**: The result screen appears — a rendered before/after of her living room. Warm tones, sage accent pillow, terracotta rug, timber bookshelf. She looks up from the phone. Looks at her actual room. Looks back. Small smile — not a huge reaction, just the quiet click of *yes.*  
She starts scrolling the product list. A card: **Rattan storage basket — AliExpress — AUD $28.**  
**Audio**: Music swells softly. No VO.  
**Visual**: The rendered room image should feel achievable — warm, put-together, but clearly the same floor plan as the before.

---

### Scene 7 — END CARD `(0:27 – 0:30)`
**Shot**: Cream card. Logo centre. Single line below.  
**Visual**:  
```
[Arch mark] Roomly

Design a room you love. On your budget.
```
**Audio**: Music resolves cleanly. Optional soft CTA VO: *"Try Roomly free."*

## Visual Direction

### Location
- **Type**: Compact rental living room or studio — 30–45 sqm
- **Feel**: Pre-makeover is *functional but impersonal*. IKEA KALLAX, generic grey couch, blank walls, mismatched rug. Lived-in but beige.
- **Avoid**: Luxury finishes, feature walls, marble, exposed brick (too stylised). This must feel like somewhere 60% of viewers actually live.

### Lighting
- Natural window light as key (afternoon/golden hour preferred)
- Warm practical light (floor lamp, 2700K) as fill
- No hard studio lighting — this is someone's home, not a set
- Phone screen should be visible and readable without over-exposing face

### Camera
- Primary: Sony FX3 or BMPCC 6K with vintage glass (Leica R or Helios for slight character)
- Handheld, slightly loose — not shaky, just alive
- Focal lengths: 35mm wide scenes, 85mm for face/phone inserts
- Shallow DOF on all phone screen and product close-ups (f/1.8–2.8)
- **No drone. No crane. No fast push-ins.** Everything feels intimate.

### Colour Grade
- Base: Warm, cream-heavy. Pull shadows slightly terracotta.
- Highlights: Creamy (#F7F3ED range), never blown
- Skin tones: Natural, warm, not over-saturated
- Shadows: Charcoal-warm, not blue or cool
- The overall feel should match Roomly's brand palette on-screen

### Talent Direction
- Late 20s woman. Naturally stylish without trying. Comfortable in her own skin.
- Genuine micro-reactions — not acting "amazed," just quietly satisfied
- **No posing.** Every moment is action-driven (looking, tapping, scrolling, sitting).
- Wardrobe: Soft, warm tones — cream, dusty terracotta, sage. Nothing branded or distracting.

---

## VO Direction

**Voice**: Female, 28–34yo. Warm and direct — not performative, not corporate. Like a friend texting you a recommendation.  
**Delivery**: Conversational, unhurried. Never excited in a forced way.  
**Pace**: Slightly slower than typical ad VO — let visuals breathe.  
**Avoid**: Upspeak, hard sells, over-enthusiasm, filler excitement words ("amazing," "incredible," "transform your life").

---

## Music Direction

**Mood**: Warm, domestic, a little cinematic. Like something you'd hear in a well-crafted travel documentary.  
**Reference feel**: Nils Frahm (light piano), Ólafur Arnalds (minimal ambient), Agnes Obel  
**Structure**: Opens sparse (just room tone + ambient). Piano enters at Scene 2. Builds gently through quiz. Swells briefly at reveal (Scene 6). Resolves cleanly at end card.  
**Tempo**: ~72–80 BPM. Nothing with a strong 4/4 beat — this isn't a tech ad.  
**Avoid**: Synth-pop, ukulele "quirky" tracks, anything that signals "startup ad."

## Production Notes

1. **App must be real and live** during filming. No Figma mockups composited in post — the UI captures (Section B) must come from the actual working app to ensure authenticity.
2. **The room must feel rented.** No custom paint, no feature wall. Standard rental white or off-white walls. This is non-negotiable for brand truth.
3. **All products in the "after" render must be purchasable** within the stated budget at time of filming. Screenshot the live prices.
4. **No. Fake. Smiles.** The talent direction is action-first, reaction-second. She does something, she reacts naturally. Brief the director accordingly.
5. **Brand colours on screen.** Any overlay text, UI, or graphic elements in post must use Roomly's design tokens: terracotta `#C7684A`, cream `#F7F3ED`, charcoal `#1A1614`, sage `#7B8B6F`.
